# Insurance Claim Form — Generation Guidance

## Document Type
Property/casualty insurance claim form. 2-3 pages. Mix of structured fields and narrative description of loss.

## Visual Style
- Insurance company header with logo placeholder, company name, claims department address
- Claim information block: claim number, policy number, date of loss, date reported
- Insured party section: name, address, phone, email, policy type
- Loss details section: structured fields (location, type of loss, estimated amount) plus a narrative description (2-4 paragraphs)
- Itemized loss schedule: table of damaged/lost items with description, age, replacement cost, actual cash value
- Adjuster notes section: 1-2 paragraphs of adjuster observations
- Signature blocks for insured and adjuster with dates
- Professional but form-like layout — mix of filled fields and narrative boxes

## Data Realism
- Claim numbers: format CLM-YYYY-NNNNN
- Policy numbers: format POL-NNNNNNN
- Types of loss: Fire, Water Damage, Theft, Wind/Hail, Vehicle Collision, Vandalism, Slip and Fall
- Loss descriptions should be 2-4 paragraphs of detailed narrative:
  - What happened, when, how it was discovered
  - Immediate actions taken
  - Extent of damage observed
- Itemized losses: 3-10 items with realistic descriptions and values
  - "Samsung 65-inch Smart TV, purchased 2022, replacement cost $899, ACV $650"
  - "Hardwood flooring, living room 400 sq ft, replacement cost $4,800, ACV $3,200"
- Estimated total loss: $1,000-$100,000 range
- Deductible: $500, $1,000, or $2,500 typical

## Math Rules
- Total replacement cost = sum of all item replacement costs
- Total ACV = sum of all item actual cash values
- Amount claimed = total ACV - deductible (or total replacement cost - deductible depending on policy)

## Common Issues to Avoid
- Loss description too brief — should read like a real claim narrative
- Inconsistent dates (date of loss after date reported)
- Items that don't match the type of loss (e.g., water damage claim listing stolen jewelry)
