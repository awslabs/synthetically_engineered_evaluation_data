# Commercial Lease Agreement — Generation Guidance

## Document Type
Commercial real estate lease agreement. 4-8 pages. The longest and most text-heavy document type. Primarily legal prose with key extractable fields embedded throughout.

## Visual Style
- Title page or prominent header: "COMMERCIAL LEASE AGREEMENT"
- Parties identified at top: Landlord and Tenant with full legal names and addresses
- Numbered sections/articles (Article 1: Premises, Article 2: Term, etc.)
- Legal prose in 10pt serif font, single-spaced, justified
- Key terms (rent amount, dates, addresses) embedded within paragraphs — not in separate fields
- Signature block at end with notary acknowledgment section
- Page numbers in footer
- Dense legal text — this should feel like a real lease

## Data Realism
- Landlord: realistic LLC or property management company name
- Tenant: realistic business name matching the property use
- Property address: realistic commercial address with suite/unit number
- Lease term: 1-5 years with specific start and end dates
- Monthly rent: $1,500-$25,000 depending on property size
- Security deposit: typically 1-2 months rent
- Common area maintenance (CAM) charges: $200-$2,000/month
- Permitted use: specific business type (e.g., "retail sales of sporting goods", "professional office services")

## Required Sections (each should be 1-3 paragraphs of legal prose)
1. Premises — description of the leased space including address, square footage, suite number
2. Term — lease start date, end date, renewal options
3. Rent — monthly amount, due date, late fee provisions, annual escalation percentage
4. Security Deposit — amount, conditions for return, deductions
5. Use of Premises — permitted use, restrictions
6. Maintenance and Repairs — landlord vs tenant responsibilities
7. Insurance — required coverage types and minimum amounts
8. Default and Remedies — what constitutes default, cure periods, landlord remedies
9. Termination — early termination conditions, notice requirements
10. Signatures — landlord, tenant, date, witness/notary

## Key Extraction Fields
LandlordName, TenantName, PropertyAddress, LeaseStartDate, LeaseEndDate, MonthlyRent, SecurityDeposit, CAMCharges, PermittedUse, SquareFootage, AnnualEscalation

## Common Issues to Avoid
- Sections too short — each should be substantive legal prose, not one-liners
- Inconsistent terms (e.g., different rent amounts in different sections)
- Missing standard lease provisions
- Total document under 3 pages — real commercial leases are lengthy
